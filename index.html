Anyone a reader

For me, reading has various uses and outlets. One of its most prevalent applications is that it allows for further growth, development, and overall success in school and life. But it also opens the door for adventure and expanding of one's understanding of the world, taking journeys to faraway places, exposure to new ideas, insight into oneself. Finding yourself in the story you are reading can bring comfort, freedom, validation, and even new friendships.Literature can also be a window into the world of someone else; opening the door to new experiences, thoughts, ideals, and perspective. A child who can access literature gains the key to discovery.

For eight years, I was a teacher and administrator in elementary education, working with students in grades K-6. I completed a master's degree in Reading and Literacy at the University of Utah in December 2019, and am now working as a doctoral student in Reading and Literacy at the University of Utah. For the past six years, I have privately tutored students who struggle with reading, and have seen the wonder of books come into their lives as they develop and enhance the skills they need to be successful readers.
